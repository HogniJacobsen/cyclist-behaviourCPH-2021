\begin{abstract}

\centerline{\LARGE \textbf{Abstract}} 
\ \\
In many European cities, such as Copenhagen and Amsterdam, cycling is an important part of the urban 
transportation network. But infrastructure often favors cars, resulting in unfriendly or even dangerous 
cycling infrastructure. 
\ \\

Having quantifiable data on the movement and behavior of cyclists is a crucial element in modern city 
planning and would result in friendlier urban infrastructure for cyclists. The current methods of data 
collection are reliant on the human annotation of video recordings, this is expensive, time-consuming, 
and could face challenges in reproducibility. We aim to evaluate a new methodological approach to replace 
manual annotation.
\ \\

Using video recordings of the Dybbølsbro intersection from multiple cameras, we perform object detection 
on the videos and then transform the detected object from the camera view to a birds-eye-view of the
intersection. Allowing us to view cyclists in more fine-grained details as well as at an aggregated 
level.
\ \\

Using our method we produced favorable results when compared to manual annotation.
\end{abstract}
